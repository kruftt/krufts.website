Tether's End

    Circa 2023 I used Godot to begin prototyping an ARPG inspired game entitled Tether's End. In Tether's End, players pilot remote controlled drones across the surface of an abandoned mining planet from the safety of a space station that is tethered to its equator. I implemented some core systemsm, such as the ability to save / load games, fly around, a hex-based resource scanning system, and basic ship statistics. For the planet's surface, I wrote a rudimentary terrain generator, using noise to create texture and normal maps. At the time, the project served as a good introduction to Godot and GDScript, but I've now moved on and picked this game idea back up in Bevy.

    Gameplay in Tether's End has two basic phases: being at the station or on a mission. At the station, players can access crafting facilities and a strategic map of the planet's surface. The strategic map is where mission selection occurs. Players remain on a mission until it is completed, failed, or abandoned.

    Tether's End draws inspiration from many titles in its design, such as:

    • Path of Exile: Combat + damage mechanics, crafting, balance.
    • XCOM2: Station/base management, strategic map layer.
    • Raiden: Twin-stick shooter inspired movement and weapons.
    • Noita: Modular and generative weapon + item crafting.
